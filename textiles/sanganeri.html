Sanganeri Print

"A delicate garden of fine flowers blooming on pristine white fabric."

The Delicate Flora

Originating from Sanganer, a town historically known for its pure river water which imparts a bright off-white radiance to the cotton. Unlike the dark base colors of Bagru, Sanganeri prints are characterized by incredibly fine, intricate floral motifs printed on a light background.

  • 1. The Bleach of Sun and Water The cotton fabric is washed extensively in the local flowing waters and naturally bleached for days under the harsh desert sun to achieve pure whiteness.
  • 2. Intricate Block Carving (Bunta) Master artisans carve extremely fine motifs—roses, lotuses, sunflowers, and royal lilies—into seasoned teak wood blocks with surgical precision.
  • 3. Direct Printing Using a technique called direct printing, the dark outline (rekh) is printed first, followed by the colorful filling (datta). Colors were traditionally vibrant yellows, striking reds, and indigo.
