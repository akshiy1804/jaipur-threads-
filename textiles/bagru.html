Bagru Print

"Pigments of the earth, manually stamped into existence."

Mud Resist and Natural Dyes

Practiced by the Chhipa community in the village of Bagru, this traditional block printing technique relies entirely on natural dyes and earthly elements. It is famous for its dark base colors, primarily indigo and madder red, and its exquisite 'Dabu' mud-resist process.

  • 1. Pre-treatment (Hari) The fabric is thoroughly treated with a wild mixture of cow dung, harda (myrobalan), sesame oil, and water to make it soft and naturally receptive to dyes.
  • 2. Dabu Paste Application A protective resist paste formulated from mud, lime, and tree gum is aggressively stamped onto the fabric using intricately carved teak wooden blocks.
  • 3. Dyeing and Washing The fabric is dyed, most often in deep indigo vats. The caked mud resist prevents the dye from penetrating. After aggressive washing and sun baking, the beautiful base fabric is revealed.
