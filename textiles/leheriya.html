Leheriya

"A visual symphony of the monsoon's undulating winds."

The Wave Pattern

Leheriya derives its name from the Rajasthani word 'leher', meaning wave. This unique tie-dye technique produces complex wave patterns, historically worn during the monsoon season (Saavan) as a symbol of joy and the life-giving rains.

  • 1. Diagonal Rolling The fabric is rolled diagonally from one corner to the opposite corner, forming a tight, structured coil.
  • 2. Tying the Resist The coiled fabric is tied tightly with threads at regular intervals to create the distinctive striped pattern, securing the tension.
  • 3. Layered Dyeing To create multicolored patterns, the fabric is unrolled, re-rolled from a different angle, tied, and dyed again, producing complex overlapping waves.
