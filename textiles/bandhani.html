Bandhani

"A thousand stars caught in a single yard of silk."

The Process of Tying

The term Bandhani is derived from the Sanskrit word 'Banda' which means 'to tie'. This intricate art form involves dyeing a fabric that is tied tightly with a thread at several points, producing a variety of patterns like Chandrakala, Bavan Baug, Shikari and so on; depending on the manner in which the cloth is tied.

  • 1. Tracing the Pattern The artisan traces the design directly onto the unbleached fabric using a stenciled block and fugitive colors.
  • 2. Tying (Bandhan) Skilled artisans pinch the fabric along the traced lines and tie them with continuous cotton thread.
  • 3. Dyeing The tied fabric is immersed in the dye bath. The tied areas resist the dye. Multiple colors require multiple cycles of tying and dyeing.
